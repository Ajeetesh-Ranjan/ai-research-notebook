**Project:** AI Research Notebook  
**Agent:** Revenue Research Agent  
**Date:** July 2025  
**Scope:** Top 20 players in the research notebook, document intelligence, and knowledge management space  

---

## Methodology

This report synthesizes data from multiple sources including founder interviews (Nathan Latka/GetLatka), PitchBook, Crunchbase, TechCrunch, SEC filings, company blogs, and industry estimates. For each company, we provide:

- **Public revenue** (if available)
- **Estimated revenue** (if not public, with methodology)
- **Total funding raised** (if available)
- **Valuation** (if available)
- **Estimated user base** (if available)
- **Pricing tiers**
- **Monetisation model**
- **Enterprise pricing** (if available)
- **Source links**
- **Confidence level** (High/Medium/Low)

**Rules followed:**
- No invented earnings or revenue figures.
- If revenue is unavailable, marked as "Not publicly disclosed."
- If estimating, methodology is explained.
- Facts separated from assumptions.

### 4. Monetisation Models
- **Freemium SaaS:** Notion, Obsidian, Mem, RemNote, Tana, Capacities, Craft, Logseq (planned)

- **Nonprofit + Add-ons:** Zotero
- **Big Tech Bundle:** NotebookLM (Google One AI Premium)

### 5. Market Gaps and Opportunities
- **No clear leader in "AI Research Notebook":** Notion is the workspace leader but lacks deep PDF/document intelligence. Adobe has document tools but lacks knowledge management. This creates a gap for an integrated solution.
- **Revenue concentration:** Most players are <$10M ARR, indicating a fragmented market with room for consolidation or a breakout winner.
- **Bootstrapped success:** Many successful tools (LiquidText, Readwise, Scrintal, PDF Expert) reached $1M+ ARR without VC funding, suggesting strong product-market fit in niches.
- **AI-native entrants:** Mem, Heptabase, Tana, and NotebookLM are positioning around AI-first workflows. This is the most competitive emerging segment.

## Data Limitations and Caveats
